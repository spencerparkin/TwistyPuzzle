3. Solving Twisty Puzzles

3.1 Coming up with your own solution

In a nutshell, coming up with your own solution amounts to finding enough useful sequences that can be used to work your way down through a subgroup chain to the trivial group. Of particular interest are sequences in the form of conjugates and commutators. Conjugates can often help you create new sequences from old ones. Commutators let you narrow or focus the net impact of a sequence based upon how much or little the two sequences taken in the commutator commute with one another.
